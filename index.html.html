Form reference — what “good” looks like

Target ranges from coaching and biomechanics literature. They're starting points — body types and styles vary, so treat big deviations as questions to investigate, not automatic faults. Measure with the Angle tool or Auto-track on the matching frame.

🏀 Basketball — jump shot
• Set point: elbow bent ~75–90° with the upper arm near parallel to the floor — measure elbow angle on the frame where the ball sits in the shot pocket.
• Dip/load: deeper knee and hip bend for longer range — proficient shooters sink lower before rising. Compare knee angle at deepest dip across shot distances.
• Release: ball leaves near the top of the jump, arm finishing at roughly 45–60° toward the rim; research puts good release trajectories around ~49–55° above horizontal for most mid-range shots (lower from deep).
• Follow-through: elbow reaches full extension (~180°) — freeze the frame just after release and check.
• Balance: jump and land near the same spot — use the Line tool as a vertical reference from the takeoff point.
• Defensive stance: feet shoulder-width or a bit wider, knees bent to roughly 100–130°, butt down and back (not leaning forward), chest up, weight on the balls of the feet, hands active around waist height. Freeze a stance frame and measure the knee angle.
• Defensive slide: short, choppy steps — push off the trail leg, the lead leg reaches out, feet stay close to (not fully crossing) each other. The stance should hold the whole time — standing up between slides is the most common breakdown coaches flag. Film 10s from the front, run Auto-track, and read the clip summary: average knee angle is stance depth, and it counts every time the athlete rises out of it (>150° knee angle).
• Push-off force (est.): auto-track also estimates the horizontal force of each slide push-off from hip movement and the athlete's bodyweight — the same physics-estimate approach as the jump timing tool. Useful for tracking whether push-off power is improving, not a lab measurement.
• Standard drills (from coaching literature): Lane slides — slide baseline to baseline across the key without crossing feet; Zig-zag / mirror drill — partner-led change-of-direction slides; Closeout — sprint out under control, chop feet, hands up, settle into stance; Stance holds — hold the position for time to build strength and habit.
🏃 Track — sprint & explosiveness
• Acceleration (first 10m): whole-body lean ~45° with shins angled forward at push-off — measure shin-to-ground with the Angle tool.
• Max velocity posture: tall and upright, thigh rising to near parallel with the ground (front-side mechanics) — measure hip angle at peak knee drive.
• Ground contact: foot strikes under the hips, not out in front — Line tool vertical from the hip; use Timing & force sprint-step mode for contact time.
• Arms: elbows near ~90°, driving from the shoulder.
• Vertical jump: countermovement to roughly 80–110° of knee bend, then full hip-knee-ankle extension at takeoff — pair the knee angle with Timing & force jump mode.
⚽ Soccer — instep kick
• Plant foot: lands beside the ball, pointing at the target — Line tool from plant foot through the ball.
• Backswing: kicking knee flexes deeply (past ~90°) before whipping to near-full extension at contact — measure knee angle at deepest cock and again at ball contact.
• Hip drive: the thigh decelerates as the shin whips through — the knee-angle change between those two frames is the power signature.
• Trunk: slight lean over the ball keeps shots down.
🏈 Football — stance & first step
• Stance: hips loaded with knees around 90–110°, back flat — measure knee and hip angle in the frozen stance frame.
• First step: positive shin angle (~45°) driving forward, pads staying low — same acceleration mechanics as the sprint start.
• Change of direction: on cuts, watch the knee tracking over the foot rather than caving inward — the AI skeleton makes a knock-kneed collapse easy to spot from a front-on clip.
• Contact time: use Timing & force sprint-step mode on the plant step of a cut — quicker off the ground with the same angle is progress.
